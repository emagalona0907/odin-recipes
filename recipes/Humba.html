Humba Recipe

Humbang Baboy Recipe

Humba is a savory Filipino dish, similar to adobo but with a sweet twist. Originating from the Visayas region, it features pork (usually belly) braised in a flavorful sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and spices, combined with brown sugar, banana, and sometimes pineapple, giving it a rich, sweet, and tangy profile. It's often served with rice, making it a comforting and hearty meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 banana, sliced (optional)
  • 1/2 cup pineapple chunks (optional)
  • 2-3 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the pork in soy sauce, garlic, and black peppercorns for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat and saut√© the onions until soft.
  3. Add the marinated pork (reserve the marinade) and brown on all sides.
  4. Pour in the vinegar and let it simmer without stirring for a few minutes.
  5. Add the reserved marinade, water, bay leaves, brown sugar, and optional banana and pineapple.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 1 hour, or until the pork is tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice, garnished with green onions if desired.
