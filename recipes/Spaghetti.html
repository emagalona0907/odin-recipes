Filipino-Style Spaghetti

Filipino-style spaghetti is a unique take on the classic Italian dish, characterized by its sweet, tangy sauce and the addition of hotdogs or sliced meats. Often topped with shredded cheese, this beloved comfort food is a staple at birthday parties and festive gatherings in the Philippines. The combination of flavors—sweet banana ketchup, savory ground meat, and the richness of cheese—creates a delightful and comforting dish that appeals to both kids and adults.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb spaghetti noodles
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lb ground beef or pork
  • 1 cup hotdogs, sliced
  • 1 cup banana ketchup
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce (optional)
  • 1-2 tbsp sugar (to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or processed cheese)
  • Chopped parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook spaghetti noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat cooking oil over medium heat and sauté onions until translucent.
  3. Add garlic and cook until fragrant.
  4. Add ground meat and cook until browned.
  5. Stir in sliced hotdogs and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Add banana ketchup, tomato sauce, water, soy sauce (if using), sugar, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  7. Simmer for about 15-20 minutes, adjusting sweetness and seasoning to taste.
  8. Combine the sauce with the cooked spaghetti noodles, tossing to coat evenly.
  9. Serve hot, topped with shredded cheese and garnished with parsley if desired.
