Adobo Recipe

Adobong Baboy Pinoy Recipe

Adobo is a beloved Filipino dish known for its savory and tangy flavor. Typically made with meat—commonly chicken or pork—it is marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns, then simmered until tender. The result is a rich, aromatic dish often served with rice, showcasing the perfect balance of salty and sour that defines Filipino cuisine. Each region and family may have their own variations, making adobo a versatile and cherished staple.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (preferably cane or white vinegar)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 2-3 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 tbsp sugar (optional, for sweetness)
  • Chopped green onions or fried garlic for garnish (optional)

Steps in cooking

  1. Marinate the pork in soy sauce, garlic, and black peppercorns for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat and sauté the onions until translucent.
  3. Add the marinated pork (reserve the marinade) and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Pour in the vinegar and let it simmer without stirring for a few minutes to cook off the acidity.
  5. Add the reserved marinade, water, bay leaves, and sugar (if using).
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 40-60 minutes, or until the pork is tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and adjust sweetness if desired.
  8. Serve hot over steamed rice, garnished with green onions or fried garlic if desired.
